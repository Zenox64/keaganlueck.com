5 particle theory rules

  1. All Matter is composed of particles
  2. The particles of one substance are the same. The particles of a different substance are made of different particles.
  3. There are attracticle forces between particles, the closer they are the stronger the force of attraction
  4. Particles are always moving
  5. The more energy particles have, the faster they move and the higher their temperature.

If energy is added or taken away, the substance can change its state.

All matter in the universe can be classified into two basic catgories:

  1. Mixtures: Matter that consists of two or more substances mixed together but not chemicall bonded. Ex-> Concrete, sugar water, cereal and milk
    • Homogeneous mixture: identical properties throughout the object. Ex-> tap water, sugar/milk, saltware.
    • Heterogenous mixture: different properties at different locations through the object. Ex-> concrete, pizza, soup
  2. Pure Subtances: Matter that consists of one substance on its own or chemically bonded together. Ex-> Gold, pure water, carbon dioxide.

Elements: simplest pure substance. Cannot be chemically altered into smaller substances.

  • The smallest part of an element that still posses the properties of the element is an atom.

Periodic table: Each element can be found on the periodic table. Each element has a chemical symbol.

Compounds: Pure substances that are not always single elements. Pure substances that are made up of more than one element. ex-> C02 H20.

The properties of a compound: properties of a compound are different than the individual elements that it makes up. Ex-> Salt NaCl.

  • Na: Sodium is a metal that is explosive in water.
  • Cl: a green, posionous gas.

    • These two combined make table salt that we use daily.

Classification of matter flow chart.

Solid: definite shape and volume.

Liquid: definite volume but changes shape.

Gas: no definite shape or volume.
